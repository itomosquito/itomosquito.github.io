About Jonathan

I am a computer scientist with interdisciplinary research interests in human judgment and decision making, cognitive science, psychology, cognitive modeling, and machine learning. In particular, my research interests revolve around developing accurate computational models of the human decision process.

In July of 2013 I accepted a position at Google and am currently based in their Los Angeles office.

I graduated in 2013 with a PhD in Computer Science from the University of Southern California under the advisement of Dr. Stacy Marsella at the Institue for Creative Science. My PhD thesis was titled: Context Dependent Utility: An Appraisal Based Approach for Modeling Context, Framing, and Decisions.
